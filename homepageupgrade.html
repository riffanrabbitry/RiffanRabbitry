History

The Netherland Dwarf is one of the smallest rabbit breeds, originating in the Netherlands in the early 20th century. Through selective breeding of smaller Polish rabbits and wild rabbits, breeders created this distinctive tiny breed that quickly gained popularity worldwide for its compact size and adorable appearance.

Description

Netherland Dwarfs are known for their compact bodies, short ears, and large heads relative to their body size. They typically weigh between 1.1 to 2.5 pounds. Despite their small size, they have bold personalities and come in a wide variety of colors including black, blue, chocolate, lilac, and many more. Their tiny size makes them popular as house pets, but they require careful handling due to their fragile bones. They're intelligent, active, and can be litter-trained relatively easily.

Rabbit Care Guide

Proper care ensures your rabbit lives a long, healthy, and happy life. Follow these essential guidelines.

Housing

Rabbits need spacious, clean housing with room to hop and stretch. Indoor housing is safer than outdoor. Provide hiding spots and chew toys.

Nutrition

80% hay, 15% fresh vegetables, 5% pellets. Unlimited fresh water. Avoid sugary treats and human foods that can harm their digestive system.

Healthcare

Annual vet checkups, regular nail trimming, dental care, and spaying/neutering. Watch for signs of illness and act promptly.

Social Needs

Rabbits are social animals. They need daily interaction, mental stimulation, and ideally, a rabbit companion for optimal wellbeing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I consider before getting a rabbit?

Rabbits are a 7-10 year commitment requiring daily care. Consider space, time, budget for food/vet care, and whether you want one or two (they're social). Research breed characteristics and ensure all household members are committed.

How do I choose the right breed for my family?

Consider your living space, experience level, and desired temperament. Netherland Dwarfs are great for apartments but need careful handling. Holland Lops are friendly family pets. Larger breeds like Flemish Giants need more space but are extremely gentle.

Do you ship rabbits internationally?

We primarily serve Indonesia with delivery available to major cities. International shipping is available to select countries with proper health certifications and following all import regulations. Contact us for specific country inquiries.

What support do you offer after purchase?

We provide lifetime support including a comprehensive care guide, 24/7 emergency advice line, discounted vet referrals, and access to our private owner community. We're committed to the lifelong wellbeing of every rabbit we breed.
